Connecting Nouns with 是 (shì)

[cite: 111-120] The word 是 (shì) is used to link two nouns. It is not used with adjectives or action verbs. [cite: 162-167]

我是学生。[cite: 121-123]
wǒ shì xuésheng.
I am a student.
他不是医生。[cite: 149-151]
tā bú shì yīshēng.
He is not a doctor.
北京是中国的首都。[cite: 143-145]
Běijīng shì Zhōngguó de shǒudū.
Beijing is the capital of China.
你是不是老师？[cite: 153-157]
nǐ shì bú shì lǎoshī?
Are you a teacher?

Sentences with Adjectival Predicates

[cite: 175-178] Adjectives describe the nature or state, usually following the adverb of degree 很 (hěn).

他很高兴。
Tā hěn gāoxìng.
He is happy.
我不饿。[cite: 183-184]
Wǒ bú è.
I'm not hungry.
今天天气很好。
Jīntiān tiānqì hěn hǎo.
The weather is good today.

Pronunciation Rules for 不 (bù/bú)

[cite: 378] The pronunciation of "不" changes depending on the tone of the word that follows it.

Condition Pronunciation Examples
[cite: 379] General case
(When NOT followed by a 4th tone word)		 bù 不高 (bù gāo) - not tall
不好 (bù hǎo) - not good
不忙 (bù máng) - not busy
[cite: 379] When followed by a 4th tone word bú 不对 (bú duì) - not correct
不是 (bú shì) - is not
不要 (bú yào) - don't want
不去 (bú qù) - not go
不错 (bú cuò) - not bad / pretty good

Usages of 在 (zài)

[cite: 187-194] 在 (zài) can function as both a verb and a preposition.

1. As a Verb (Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place [cite: 195-197]
我在上海。[cite: 198-199]
Wǒ zài Shànghǎi.
I'm in Shanghai.
老板在外面。[cite: 201-202]
Lǎobǎn zài wàimiàn.
The boss is outside.
他不在学校。[cite: 203-204]
Tā bú zài xuéxiào.
He's not at school.

2. As a Preposition (Action Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place + Verb [cite: 213-216]
我在朋友家喝茶。[cite: 218-220]
Wǒ zài péngyou jiā hē chá.
I drink tea at a friend's house.
他们在学校看书。[cite: 221-223]
Tāmen zài xuéxiào kàn shū.
They read books at school.
我在家看电视。
Wǒ zài jiā kàn diànshì.
I watch TV at home.

Time Expressions

[cite: 243-245, 294-295] In Chinese, time is strictly expressed from largest unit to smallest: Hour → Minute.

Expressing Clock Time

O'clock: Number + 点 (diǎn). *2 o'clock is 两点 (liǎng diǎn). [cite: 298-303]
Minutes: Number + 分 (fēn). If under 10, add 零 (líng). [cite: 304-309]
Half past: Number + 点半 (diǎn bàn). [cite: 310-312]
现在三点。
Xiànzài sān diǎn.
It's 3 o'clock now.
现在五点二十。
Xiànzài wǔ diǎn èrshí fēn.
It's 5:20 now.
现在两点零五分。[cite: 308-309]
Xiànzài liǎng diǎn líng wǔ fēn.
It's 2:05 now.
现在六点半。
Xiànzài liù diǎn bàn.
It's 6:30 now.

Sentences with Time

Time + Subject + Predicate OR Subject + Time + Predicate [cite: 331-332, 350]
今天我去学校。[cite: 277-280]
Jīntiān wǒ qù xuéxiào.
I go to school today.
昨天晚上我看电视。[cite: 285-287]
Zuótiān wǎnshang wǒ kàn diànshì.
I watched TV yesterday evening.
早上八点上课。[cite: 322-324]
Zǎoshang bā diǎn shàngkè.
Class starts at 8 a.m.

Chinese Modal Verbs

Modal verbs change the tone or necessity of an action.

1. 会 (huì) - Learned Skill / Ability

[cite: 272-274] Expresses a skill or ability acquired through learning or practice.

我会说汉语。[cite: 276-278]
Wǒ huì shuō Hànyǔ.
I can speak Chinese.
她会做中国菜。[cite: 279-281]
Tā huì zuò Zhōngguó cài.
She can cook Chinese food.
他不会写汉字。[cite: 282-284]
Tā bú huì xiě Hànzì.
He cannot write Chinese characters.
你会不会打篮球？[cite: 285-287]
Nǐ huì bú huì dǎ lánqiú?
Can you play basketball or not?
我会开车。[cite: 288-290]
Wǒ huì kāichē.
I can drive a car.

2. 会 (huì) - Possibility / Future Prediction

[cite: 293-294] Expresses likelihood or probability of something happening.

明天会下雨。[cite: 296-298]
Míngtiān huì xiàyǔ.
It will rain tomorrow.
北京冬天会很冷。[cite: 299-301]
Běijīng dōngtiān huì hěn lěng.
Beijing will be very cold in winter.
他会来。[cite: 302-304]
Tā huì lái.
He will come.
价格会有变动。[cite: 305-307]
Jiàgé huì yǒu biàndòng.
The prices are subject to change.
人人都会犯错误。[cite: 308-310]
Rénrén dōu huì fàn cuòwù.
Every man makes mistakes.

3. 想 (xiǎng) - Desire / Plan

[cite: 312-315] Expresses desire, intention, or plan. Softer than "要(yào)," closer to "would like to."

我想喝茶。[cite: 317-319]
Wǒ xiǎng hē chá.
I'd like to drink tea.
她想去中国。[cite: 320-322]
Tā xiǎng qù Zhōngguó.
She wants to go to China.
我想买这本书。[cite: 323-324]
Wǒ xiǎng mǎi zhè běn shū.
I want to buy this book.
他们想在周末去看电影。[cite: 325, 328-329]
Tāmen xiǎng zài zhōumò qù kàn diànyǐng.
They want to watch a movie on the weekend.
我想学弹钢琴。[cite: 330-332]
Wǒ xiǎng xué tán gāngqín.
I want to learn to play the piano.

4. 要 (yào) - Strong Intention / Necessity

[cite: 334-337] Expresses strong intention, determination, necessity, or an imminent action. Stronger than "想(xiǎng)."

我要喝水。[cite: 339-341]
Wǒ yào hē shuǐ.
I want to drink water.
我们要和平，不要战争。[cite: 342-344]
Wǒmen yào hépíng, bú yào zhànzhēng.
We desire peace, not war.
我要去北京工作。[cite: 345-346]
Wǒ yào qù Běijīng gōngzuò.
I'm going to work in Beijing.
要下雨了。[cite: 348-350]
Yào xiàyǔ le.
It's going to rain.
我明天要见朋友。[cite: 351-353]
Wǒ míngtiān yào jiàn péngyou.
I'm going to meet a friend tomorrow.
我们要准备考试。[cite: 355, 357-358]
Wǒmen yào zhǔnbèi kǎoshì.
We need to prepare for the exam.

5. 能 (néng) - Ability / Permission

[cite: 359-361] Expresses ability, possibility, or permission. Emphasizes whether conditions allow.

我能坐这儿吗？[cite: 363-365]
Wǒ néng zuò zhèr ma?
Can I sit here?
他今天不能来上课。[cite: 366-368]
Tā jīntiān bù néng lái shàngkè.
He cannot come to class today.
你能帮我吗？[cite: 369-371]
Nǐ néng bāng wǒ ma?
Can you help me?
我们明天能见面吗？[cite: 372-374]
Wǒmen míngtiān néng jiànmiàn ma?
Can we meet tomorrow?
你能说大声一点吗？[cite: 375-378]
Nǐ néng shuō dàshēng yìdiǎn ma?
Can you speak louder?
