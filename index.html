Connecting Nouns with 是 (shì)

The word 是 (shì) is used to link two nouns. It is not used with adjectives or action verbs.

我是学生。
wǒ shì xuésheng.
I am a student.
他不是医生。
tā bú shì yīshēng.
He is not a doctor.
北京是中国的首都。
Běijīng shì Zhōngguó de shǒudū.
Beijing is the capital of China.
你是不是老师？
nǐ shì bú shì lǎoshī?
Are you a teacher?

Sentences with Adjectival Predicates

Adjectives describe the nature or state, usually following the adverb of degree 很 (hěn).

他很高兴。
Tā hěn gāoxìng.
He is happy.
我不饿。
Wǒ bú è.
I'm not hungry.
今天天气很好。
Jīntiān tiānqì hěn hǎo.
The weather is good today.

Pronunciation Rules for 不 (bù/bú)

The pronunciation of "不" changes depending on the tone of the word that follows it.

Condition Pronunciation Examples
General case
(When NOT followed by a 4th tone word)		 bù 不高 (bù gāo) - not tall
不好 (bù hǎo) - not good
不忙 (bù máng) - not busy
When followed by a 4th tone word bú 不对 (bú duì) - not correct
不是 (bú shì) - is not
不要 (bú yào) - don't want
不去 (bú qù) - not go
不错 (bú cuò) - not bad / pretty good

Usages of 在 (zài)

在 (zài) can function as both a verb and a preposition.

1. As a Verb (Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place
我在上海。
Wǒ zài Shànghǎi.
I'm in Shanghai.
老板在外面。
Lǎobǎn zài wàimiàn.
The boss is outside.
他不在学校。
Tā bú zài xuéxiào.
He's not at school.

2. As a Preposition (Action Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place + Verb
我在朋友家喝茶。
Wǒ zài péngyou jiā hē chá.
I drink tea at a friend's house.
他们在学校看书。
Tāmen zài xuéxiào kàn shū.
They read books at school.
我在家看电视。
Wǒ zài jiā kàn diànshì.
I watch TV at home.

Time Expressions

In Chinese, time is strictly expressed from largest unit to smallest: Hour → Minute.

Expressing Clock Time

O'clock: Number + 点 (diǎn). *2 o'clock is 两点 (liǎng diǎn).
Minutes: Number + 分 (fēn). If under 10, add 零 (líng).
Half past: Number + 点半 (diǎn bàn).
现在三点。
Xiànzài sān diǎn.
It's 3 o'clock now.
现在五点二十。
Xiànzài wǔ diǎn èrshí fēn.
It's 5:20 now.
现在两点零五分。
Xiànzài liǎng diǎn líng wǔ fēn.
It's 2:05 now.
现在六点半。
Xiànzài liù diǎn bàn.
It's 6:30 now.

Sentences with Time

Time + Subject + Predicate OR Subject + Time + Predicate
今天我去学校。
Jīntiān wǒ qù xuéxiào.
I go to school today.
昨天晚上我看电视。
Zuótiān wǎnshang wǒ kàn diànshì.
I watched TV yesterday evening.
早上八点上课。
Zǎoshang bā diǎn shàngkè.
Class starts at 8 a.m.

Chinese Modal Verbs

Modal verbs change the tone or necessity of an action.

1. 会 (huì) - Learned Skill / Ability

我会说汉语。
Wǒ huì shuō Hànyǔ.
I can speak Chinese.
他不会写汉字。
Tā bú huì xiě Hànzì.
He cannot write Chinese characters.

2. 会 (huì) - Possibility / Future Prediction

明天会下雨。
Míngtiān huì xiàyǔ.
It will rain tomorrow.
他会来。
Tā huì lái.
He will come.

3. 想 (xiǎng) - Desire / Plan

我想喝茶。
Wǒ xiǎng hē chá.
I'd like to drink tea.
我想买这本书。
Wǒ xiǎng mǎi zhè běn shū.
I want to buy this book.

4. 要 (yào) - Strong Intention / Necessity

我要喝水。
Wǒ yào hē shuǐ.
I want to drink water.
我要去北京工作。
Wǒ yào qù Běijīng gōngzuò.
I'm going to work in Beijing.

5. 能 (néng) - Ability / Permission

我能坐这儿吗？
Wǒ néng zuò zhèr ma?
Can I sit here?
你能帮我吗？
Nǐ néng bāng wǒ ma?
Can you help me?

Chinese Question Words (中文疑问词)

In Chinese, question words are placed exactly where the answer would be in the sentence. The word order does not change!

1. 什么 (shénme) - What

这是什么？
Zhè shì shénme?
What is this?
你吃什么？
Nǐ chī shénme?
What do you eat?

2. 谁 (shéi / shuí) - Who

他是谁？
Tā shì shéi?
Who is he?
这个手机是谁的？
Zhège shǒujī shì shéi de?
Whose cellphone is this?

3. 哪里 / 哪儿 (nǎlǐ / nǎr) - Where

你在哪里？
Nǐ zài nǎlǐ?
Where are you?
洗手间在哪里？
Xǐshǒujiān zài nǎlǐ?
Where is the restroom?

4. 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) - When

我们什么时候吃饭？
Wǒmen shénme shíhou chīfàn?
When do we eat?
电影什么时候开始？
Diànyǐng shénme shíhou kāishǐ?
When does the movie start?

5. The Difference: 为什么 (Why) vs. 怎么 (How / How come)

你为什么生气？
Nǐ wèi shénme shēngqì?
Why are you angry? (Reason)
这个汉字怎么写？
Zhège Hànzì zěnme xiě?
How do you write this character? (Method)
你怎么不吃饭？
Nǐ zěnme bù chīfàn?
How come you aren't eating? (Surprise)

Actions in Progress: 在...呢 (zài...ne)

To express that an action is currently in progress (similar to "-ing" in English), place the adverb 在 (zài) or 正在 (zhèngzài) before the verb. You can also add the modal particle 呢 (ne) at the end of the sentence to emphasize the continuing state.

💡 Good to know (Is "呢" optional?): Yes! The 呢 (ne) is completely optional. "他在看书" and "他在看书呢" both mean "He is reading." Adding "呢" just makes the tone a bit softer and slightly more conversational.

Affirmative Form (Subject + 在/正在 + Verb + Object + 呢)

喂，你在做什么呢？
Wèi, nǐ zài zuò shénme ne?
Hello, what are you doing?
我在看书呢。
Wǒ zài kàn shū ne.
I am reading a book.
小王在学习汉语。
Xiǎo Wáng zài xuéxí Hànyǔ.
Xiao Wang is studying Chinese. (Without 呢)
我正在睡觉呢。
Wǒ zhèngzài shuìjiào ne.
I am sleeping right now.
妈妈在做饭。
Māma zài zuòfàn.
Mom is cooking.
我们正在听音乐。
Wǒmen zhèngzài tīng yīnyuè.
We are listening to music.

Negative Form (Subject + 没在 + Verb)

To say someone is not doing something right now, use 没在 (méi zài) or just 没 (méi) before the verb.
Crucial Rule: Do not use "呢" at the end of negative sentences.

我没在看电视。
Wǒ méi zài kàn diànshì.
I am not watching TV.
他们没在工作。
Tāmen méi zài gōngzuò.
They are not working.
他没看书。
Tā méi kàn shū.
He is not reading.
李老师没在睡觉。
Lǐ lǎoshī méi zài shuìjiào.
Teacher Li is not sleeping.

Indicating Occurrence or Completion: 了 (le)

The particle 了 (le) is used to indicate that an action has been completed or that a new situation/state has occurred.

1. "了" at the End of a Sentence

When placed at the very end of a sentence, it often signals a completed action or a change of state.

我去商店了。
Wǒ qù shāngdiàn le.
I went to the store.
他去学开车了。
Tā qù xué kāichē le.
He went to learn how to drive.
你买什么了？
Nǐ mǎi shénme le?
What did you buy?
昨天下雨了。
Zuótiān xiàyǔ le.
It rained yesterday.
李老师去学校了。
Lǐ lǎoshī qù xuéxiào le.
Teacher Li went to school.

2. "了" Between the Verb and Object

When "了" is placed directly after the verb and before the object, the object usually needs a modifier (like a number, a measure word, an adjective, or a pronoun).

她买了一点儿苹果。
Tā mǎi le yìdiǎnr píngguǒ.
She bought a few apples.
我买了不少衣服。
Wǒ mǎi le bùshǎo yīfu.
I bought quite a few clothes.
你看见了几个人？
Nǐ kànjiàn le jǐ ge rén?
How many people did you see?
我喝了一杯咖啡。
Wǒ hē le yì bēi kāfēi.
I drank a cup of coffee.

3. Negative Form: 没 (méi) + Verb (Drop the "了"!)

⚠️ Crucial Rule: To say you didn't do something, use 没 (méi) or 没有 (méi yǒu) before the verb. You MUST omit the "了" in negative sentences!
她没去商店。
Tā méi qù shāngdiàn.
She didn't go to the store.
我没买衣服。
Wǒ méi mǎi yīfu.
I didn't buy clothes.
我没看见张先生。
Wǒ méi kànjiàn Zhāng xiānsheng.
I didn't see Mr. Zhang.
他没去学开车。
Tā méi qù xué kāichē.
He didn't go to learn driving.
