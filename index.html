Connecting Nouns with 是 (shì)

The word 是 (shì) is used to link two nouns. It is not used with adjectives or action verbs.

我是学生。
wǒ shì xuésheng.
I am a student.
他不是医生。
tā bú shì yīshēng.
He is not a doctor.
北京是中国的首都。
Běijīng shì Zhōngguó de shǒudū.
Beijing is the capital of China.
你是不是老师？
nǐ shì bú shì lǎoshī?
Are you a teacher?

Sentences with Adjectival Predicates

Adjectives describe the nature or state, usually following the adverb of degree 很 (hěn).

他很高兴。
Tā hěn gāoxìng.
He is happy.
我不饿。
Wǒ bú è.
I'm not hungry.
今天天气很好。
Jīntiān tiānqì hěn hǎo.
The weather is good today.

Pronunciation Rules for 不 (bù/bú)

The pronunciation of "不" changes depending on the tone of the word that follows it.

Condition Pronunciation Examples
General case
(When NOT followed by a 4th tone word)		 bù 不高 (bù gāo) - not tall
不好 (bù hǎo) - not good
不忙 (bù máng) - not busy
When followed by a 4th tone word bú 不对 (bú duì) - not correct
不是 (bú shì) - is not
不要 (bú yào) - don't want
不去 (bú qù) - not go
不错 (bú cuò) - not bad / pretty good

Usages of 在 (zài)

在 (zài) can function as both a verb and a preposition.

1. As a Verb (Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place
我在上海。
Wǒ zài Shànghǎi.
I'm in Shanghai.
老板在外面。
Lǎobǎn zài wàimiàn.
The boss is outside.
他不在学校。
Tā bú zài xuéxiào.
He's not at school.

2. As a Preposition (Action Location)

Structure: Subject + 在 (zài) + Place + Verb
我在朋友家喝茶。
Wǒ zài péngyou jiā hē chá.
I drink tea at a friend's house.
他们在学校看书。
Tāmen zài xuéxiào kàn shū.
They read books at school.
我在家看电视。
Wǒ zài jiā kàn diànshì.
I watch TV at home.

Time Expressions

In Chinese, time is strictly expressed from largest unit to smallest: Hour → Minute.

Expressing Clock Time

O'clock: Number + 点 (diǎn). *2 o'clock is 两点 (liǎng diǎn).
Minutes: Number + 分 (fēn). If under 10, add 零 (líng).
Half past: Number + 点半 (diǎn bàn).
现在三点。
Xiànzài sān diǎn.
It's 3 o'clock now.
现在五点二十。
Xiànzài wǔ diǎn èrshí fēn.
It's 5:20 now.
现在两点零五分。
Xiànzài liǎng diǎn líng wǔ fēn.
It's 2:05 now.
现在六点半。
Xiànzài liù diǎn bàn.
It's 6:30 now.

Sentences with Time

Time + Subject + Predicate OR Subject + Time + Predicate
今天我去学校。
Jīntiān wǒ qù xuéxiào.
I go to school today.
昨天晚上我看电视。
Zuótiān wǎnshang wǒ kàn diànshì.
I watched TV yesterday evening.
早上八点上课。
Zǎoshang bā diǎn shàngkè.
Class starts at 8 a.m.

Chinese Modal Verbs

Modal verbs change the tone or necessity of an action.

1. 会 (huì) - Learned Skill / Ability

Expresses a skill or ability acquired through learning or practice.

我会说汉语。
Wǒ huì shuō Hànyǔ.
I can speak Chinese.
她会做中国菜。
Tā huì zuò Zhōngguó cài.
She can cook Chinese food.
他不会写汉字。
Tā bú huì xiě Hànzì.
He cannot write Chinese characters.
你会不会打篮球？
Nǐ huì bú huì dǎ lánqiú?
Can you play basketball or not?
我会开车。
Wǒ huì kāichē.
I can drive a car.

2. 会 (huì) - Possibility / Future Prediction

Expresses likelihood or probability of something happening.

明天会下雨。
Míngtiān huì xiàyǔ.
It will rain tomorrow.
北京冬天会很冷。
Běijīng dōngtiān huì hěn lěng.
Beijing will be very cold in winter.
他会来。
Tā huì lái.
He will come.
价格会有变动。
Jiàgé huì yǒu biàndòng.
The prices are subject to change.
人人都会犯错误。
Rénrén dōu huì fàn cuòwù.
Every man makes mistakes.

3. 想 (xiǎng) - Desire / Plan

Expresses desire, intention, or plan. Softer than "要(yào)," closer to "would like to."

我想喝茶。
Wǒ xiǎng hē chá.
I'd like to drink tea.
她想去中国。
Tā xiǎng qù Zhōngguó.
She wants to go to China.
我想买这本书。
Wǒ xiǎng mǎi zhè běn shū.
I want to buy this book.
他们想在周末去看电影。
Tāmen xiǎng zài zhōumò qù kàn diànyǐng.
They want to watch a movie on the weekend.
我想学弹钢琴。
Wǒ xiǎng xué tán gāngqín.
I want to learn to play the piano.

4. 要 (yào) - Strong Intention / Necessity

Expresses strong intention, determination, necessity, or an imminent action. Stronger than "想(xiǎng)."

我要喝水。
Wǒ yào hē shuǐ.
I want to drink water.
我们要和平，不要战争。
Wǒmen yào hépíng, bú yào zhànzhēng.
We desire peace, not war.
我要去北京工作。
Wǒ yào qù Běijīng gōngzuò.
I'm going to work in Beijing.
要下雨了。
Yào xiàyǔ le.
It's going to rain.
我明天要见朋友。
Wǒ míngtiān yào jiàn péngyou.
I'm going to meet a friend tomorrow.
我们要准备考试。
Wǒmen yào zhǔnbèi kǎoshì.
We need to prepare for the exam.

5. 能 (néng) - Ability / Permission

Expresses ability, possibility, or permission. Emphasizes whether conditions allow.

我能坐这儿吗？
Wǒ néng zuò zhèr ma?
Can I sit here?
他今天不能来上课。
Tā jīntiān bù néng lái shàngkè.
He cannot come to class today.
你能帮我吗？
Nǐ néng bāng wǒ ma?
Can you help me?
我们明天能见面吗？
Wǒmen míngtiān néng jiànmiàn ma?
Can we meet tomorrow?
你能说大声一点吗？
Nǐ néng shuō dàshēng yìdiǎn ma?
Can you speak louder?

Chinese Question Words (中文疑问词)

In Chinese, question words are placed exactly where the answer would be in the sentence. The word order does not change!

1. 什么 (shénme) - What

这是什么？
Zhè shì shénme?
What is this?
你吃什么？
Nǐ chī shénme?
What do you eat?
他说什么？
Tā shuō shénme?
What did he say?
你喜欢什么书？
Nǐ xǐhuan shénme shū?
What books do you like?

👀 Show Answer
你想买什么？
Nǐ xiǎng mǎi shénme?

2. 谁 (shéi / shuí) - Who

他是谁？
Tā shì shéi?
Who is he?
谁在外面？
Shéi zài wàimiàn?
Who is outside?
这个手机是谁的？
Zhège shǒujī shì shéi de?
Whose cellphone is this?
你想认识谁？
Nǐ xiǎng rènshi shéi?
Who do you want to meet?
🎯 Practice: Translate "Who is your Chinese teacher?"
👀 Show Answer
你的中文老师是谁？
Nǐ de Zhōngwén lǎoshī shì shéi?

3. 哪里 / 哪儿 (nǎlǐ / nǎr) - Where

你在哪里？
Nǐ zài nǎlǐ?
Where are you?
我的手机在哪儿？
Wǒ de shǒujī zài nǎr?
Where is my cellphone?
洗手间在哪里？
Xǐshǒujiān zài nǎlǐ?
Where is the restroom?
他们去哪里吃饭？
Tāmen qù nǎlǐ chīfàn?
Where are they going to eat?

👀 Show Answer
他在哪里工作？
Tā zài nǎlǐ gōngzuò?

4. 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) - When

我们什么时候吃饭？
Wǒmen shénme shíhou chīfàn?
When do we eat?
你什么时候睡觉？
Nǐ shénme shíhou shuìjiào?
When do you sleep?
爸爸什么时候回家？
Bàba shénme shíhou huíjiā?
When is dad coming home?
电影什么时候开始？
Diànyǐng shénme shíhou kāishǐ?
When does the movie start?
🎯 Practice: Translate "When do you go to school?"
👀 Show Answer
你什么时候去学校？
Nǐ shénme shíhou qù xuéxiào?

5. The Difference: 为什么 (Why) vs. 怎么 (How / How come)

It's easy to mix these up!
👉 为什么 (wèi shénme) formally asks for the reason or cause ("Why did this happen?").
👉 怎么 (zěnme) primarily asks for the method, process, or manner of an action ("How do I do this?").
🌟 Bonus: 怎么 (zěnme) can also be used to mean "how come" to ask for a reason. It usually expresses a tone of surprise, curiosity, or confusion!

🧱 Sentence Structure (Word Order Logic):
In English, question words like "Why" and "How" are usually pulled to the very beginning of the sentence. In Chinese, 为什么 and 怎么 act as adverbs. This means they must be placed after the subject and directly before the verb or adjective.

Subject + 为什么 / 怎么 + Verb / Adjective ?

*Literal translation: "You why angry?" (你为什么生气？) or "We how go to Beijing?" (我们怎么去北京？)

为什么 (Why - Direct Reason):
你为什么生气？
Nǐ wèi shénme shēngqì?
Why are you angry?
他为什么不来？
Tā wèi shénme bù lái?
Why isn't he coming?
你为什么学中文？
Nǐ wèi shénme xué Zhōngwén?
Why do you study Chinese?
怎么 (How - Method):
这个汉字怎么写？
Zhège Hànzì zěnme xiě?
How do you write this Chinese character?
我们怎么去北京？
Wǒmen zěnme qù Běijīng?
How do we go to Beijing?
这个怎么卖？
Zhège zěnme mài?
How is this sold? (How much is it?)
怎么 (How come - Reason + Surprise):
他怎么没来上课？
Tā zěnme méi lái shàngkè?
How come he didn't come to class?
你怎么不吃饭？
Nǐ zěnme bù chīfàn?
How come you aren't eating?
今天怎么这么热？
Jīntiān zěnme zhème rè?
How come it is so hot today?

Actions in Progress: 在...呢 (zài...ne)

To express that an action is currently in progress (similar to "-ing" in English), place the adverb 在 (zài) or 正在 (zhèngzài) before the verb. You can also add the modal particle 呢 (ne) at the end of the sentence to emphasize the continuing state.

💡 Good to know (Is "呢" optional?): Yes! The 呢 (ne) is completely optional. "他在看书" and "他在看书呢" both mean "He is reading." Adding "呢" just makes the tone a bit softer and slightly more conversational.

Affirmative Form (Subject + 在/正在 + Verb + Object + 呢)

喂，你在做什么呢？
Wèi, nǐ zài zuò shénme ne?
Hello, what are you doing?
我在看书呢。
Wǒ zài kàn shū ne.
I am reading a book.
小王在学习汉语。
Xiǎo Wáng zài xuéxí Hànyǔ.
Xiao Wang is studying Chinese. (Without 呢)
我正在睡觉呢。
Wǒ zhèngzài shuìjiào ne.
I am sleeping right now.
妈妈在做饭。
Māma zài zuòfàn.
Mom is cooking.
我们正在听音乐。
Wǒmen zhèngzài tīng yīnyuè.
We are listening to music.

Negative Form (Subject + 没在 + Verb)

To say someone is not doing something right now, use 没在 (méi zài) or just 没 (méi) before the verb.
Crucial Rule: Do not use "呢" at the end of negative sentences.

我没在看电视。
Wǒ méi zài kàn diànshì.
I am not watching TV.
他们没工作。
Tāmen méi zài gōngzuò.
They are not working.
他没看书。
Tā méi kàn shū.
He is not reading.
李老师没在睡觉。
Lǐ lǎoshī méi zài shuìjiào.
Teacher Li is not sleeping.

Indicating Occurrence or Completion: 了 (le)

The particle 了 (le) is used to indicate that an action has been completed or that a new situation/state has occurred.

1. "了" at the End of a Sentence

When placed at the very end of a sentence, it often signals a completed action or a change of state.

我去商店了。
Wǒ qù shāngdiàn le.
I went to the store.
他去学开车了。
Tā qù xué kāichē le.
He went to learn how to drive.
你买什么了？
Nǐ mǎi shénme le?
What did you buy?
昨天下雨了。
Zuótiān xiàyǔ le.
It rained yesterday.
李老师去学校了。
Lǐ lǎoshī qù xuéxiào le.
Teacher Li went to school.

2. "了" Between the Verb and Object

When "了" is placed directly after the verb and before the object, the object usually needs a modifier (like a number, a measure word, an adjective, or a pronoun).

她买了一点儿苹果。
Tā mǎi le yìdiǎnr píngguǒ.
She bought a few apples.
我买了不少衣服。
Wǒ mǎi le bùshǎo yīfu.
I bought quite a few clothes.
你看见了几个人？
Nǐ kànjiàn le jǐ ge rén?
How many people did you see?
我喝了一杯咖啡。
Wǒ hē le yì bēi kāfēi.
I drank a cup of coffee.

3. Negative Form: 没 (méi) + Verb (Drop the "了"!)

⚠️ Crucial Rule: To say you didn't do something, use 没 (méi) or 没有 (méi yǒu) before the verb. You MUST omit the "了" in negative sentences!
她没去商店。
Tā méi qù shāngdiàn.
She didn't go to the store.
我没买衣服。
Wǒ méi mǎi yīfu.
I didn't buy clothes.
我没看见张先生。
Wǒ méi kànjiàn Zhāng xiānsheng.
I didn't see Mr. Zhang.
他没去学开车。
Tā méi qù xué kāichē.
He didn't go to learn driving.

Expressing "Too": 太...了 (tài...le)

This structure is used to express that something is excessive or "too much." It often appears with adjectives.

今天天气太热了。
Jīntiān tiānqì tài rè le.
The weather is too hot today.
太好了！
Tài hǎo le!
Great! / Too good!

The Emphasis Structure: 是...的 (shì...de)

Used to emphasize a specific detail (time, place, or manner) of a past event. The focus is on how or when something happened rather than just the action.

李先生是什么时候来的？
Lǐ xiānsheng shì shénme shíhou lái de?
Exactly when did Mr. Li arrive?
我是坐飞机来的。
Wǒ shì zuò fēijī lái de.
I came by airplane.

Position of Action Measure Word "次" (cì)

In Chinese grammar, the position of "次" (times) depends entirely on the type of object that follows the verb.

1. When the Object is a Place (Location Noun)

If the object is a place (like Shanghai, Beijing, hospital), "次" can be placed either before or after the place. Both are perfectly correct and mean the exact same thing.

我去过上海两次。
Wǒ qù guò Shànghǎi liǎng cì.
I have been to Shanghai twice. (Verb + 过 + Place + 次)
我去过两次上海。
Wǒ qù guò liǎng cì Shànghǎi.
I have been to Shanghai twice. (Verb + 过 + 次 + Place)

2. When the Object is a Regular Noun

If the object is a regular noun (like movie, duck, book), "次" usually MUST be placed before the noun (acting like a quantity modifier).

我吃过两次烤鸭。
Wǒ chī guò liǎng cì kǎoyā.
✅ I have eaten roast duck twice.
我吃过烤鸭两次。
❌ Incorrect order

3. When the Object is a Personal Pronoun

If the object is a personal pronoun (like him 他, you 你, me 我), "次" MUST be strictly placed after the pronoun.

我见过他两次。
Wǒ jiàn guò tā liǎng cì.
✅ I have met him twice.
我见过两次他。
❌ Incorrect order

📏 Measure Words (量词)

Measure words are essential in Chinese. They act as "counters" placed between a number (or a demonstrative like 这 "this" / 那 "that") and a noun.

1. 个 (gè) - People & General Items

The most common measure word. If you don't know the measure word for something, use 个 as a safe guess.

他是一个好人。
Tā shì yí ge hǎo rén.
He is a good person.
我有两个中国朋友。
Wǒ yǒu liǎng ge Zhōngguó péngyou.
I have two Chinese friends.
这个苹果很大。
Zhè ge píngguǒ hěn hǎochī.
This apple is very big.

2. 本 (běn) - Books & Bound Items

我想买一本书。
Wǒ xiǎng mǎi yì běn shū.
I want to buy a book.
两本字典
liǎng běn zìdiǎn
two dictionaries
几本杂志
jǐ běn zázhì
a few magazines

3. 岁 (suì) - Age (Years Old)

一岁
yí suì
one year old
二十岁
èrshí suì
twenty years old
他几岁了？
Tā jǐ suì le?
How old is he?

4. 块 (kuài) - Pieces / Chinese Currency

一块钱
yí kuài qián
one RMB / one buck
我吃了两块蛋糕。
Wǒ chī le liǎng kuài dàngāo.
I ate two pieces of cake.
那块手表
nà kuài shǒubiǎo
that watch

5. 元 (yuán) - Formal Unit of Money

十元
shí yuán
ten yuan
一百元
yìbǎi yuán
one hundred yuan
五十元
wǔshí yuán
fifty yuan

6. 件 (jiàn) - Clothing, Events/Matters

这件衣服很漂亮。
Zhè jiàn yīfu hěn piàoliang.
This piece of clothing is very pretty.
两件毛衣
liǎng jiàn máoyī
two sweaters
一件事
yí jiàn shì
one matter/thing to do

7. 张 (zhāng) - Flat Objects (Paper, Tickets, Tables)

一张纸
yì zhāng zhǐ
a piece of paper
我们有两张电影票。
Wǒmen yǒu liǎng zhāng diànyǐng piào.
We have two movie tickets.
三张桌子
sān zhāng zhuōzi
three tables

8. 条 (tiáo) - Long, Flexible Things

这条裙子很漂亮。
zhè tiáo qúnzi hěn piàoliang.
This dress/skirt is very pretty.
我买了两条鱼。
wǒ mǎi le liǎng tiáo yú.
I bought two fish.
三条裤子
sān tiáo kùzi
three pairs of pants

9. 次 (cì) - Action Frequency (Times)

一次
yí cì
one time
去过两次
qù guò liǎng cì
went twice
看过三次
kàn guò sān cì
seen three times

10. 些 (xiē) - Some / A Few

他买了一些水果。
Tā mǎi le yìxiē shuǐguǒ.
He bought some fruit.
这些人
zhèxiē rén
these people
那些书
nàxiē shū
those books

11. 一点儿 (yīdiǎnr) - A Little Bit (Quantity)

喝一点儿水
hē yìdiǎnr shuǐ
drink a little water
吃一点儿东西
chī yìdiǎnr dōngxi
eat a little something
一点儿钱
yìdiǎnr qián
a little money

12. 一下 (yīxià) - Briefly / Casually (Follows a verb)

看一下
kàn yíxià
take a look
等一下
děng yíxià
wait a moment
休息一下
xiūxi yíxià
rest a bit

Dialogue: Traveling to Beijing

A
我要去北京旅游，你觉得什么时候去最好？
Wǒ yào qù Běijīng lǚyóu, nǐ juéde shénme shíhou qù zuì hǎo?
I'm thinking of a trip to Beijing. When do you think is the best time?
B
九月去北京旅游最好。
Jiǔ yuè qù Běijīng lǚyóu zuì hǎo.
September is the best time to visit Beijing.
A
为什么？
Wèi shénme?
Why?
B
九月的北京天气不冷也不热。
Jiǔ yuè de Běijīng tiānqì bù lěng yě bú rè.
Because it's neither cold nor hot there in September.

Key Expressions

1. 觉得 (juéde) - to think / to feel

Use 觉得 to express an opinion or a feeling.

我觉得中文很难。
Wǒ juéde Zhōngwén hěn nán.
I think Chinese is very difficult.
你觉得这个菜怎么样？
Nǐ juéde zhè ge cài zěnmeyàng?
What do you think about this dish?
我觉得今天很热。
Wǒ juéde jīntiān hěn rè.
I feel it is very hot today.

2. 最 (zuì) & 最好 (zuì hǎo) - the most / best

最 means "most". 最好 means "best" or "had better".

这里风景最好。
Zhèlǐ fēngjǐng zuì hǎo.
The scenery here is the best.
你最好明天去。
Nǐ zuì hǎo míngtiān qù.
You had better go tomorrow.
我最喜欢吃苹果。
Wǒ zuì xǐhuan chī píngguǒ.
I like eating apples the most.

3. 过 (guò) - Past Experiences

Add 过 directly after a verb to express that you have experienced an action in the past.

Structure: Subject + Verb + 过 + Object
我去过中国。
Wǒ qù guò Zhōngguó.
I have been to China.
我没吃过烤鸭。
Wǒ méi chī guò kǎoyā.
I have never eaten roast duck.
你看过这个电影吗？
Nǐ kàn guò zhè ge diànyǐng ma?
Have you seen this movie?
